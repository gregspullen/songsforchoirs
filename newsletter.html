May 2026 review

Hello Choristers,

Welcome back. Wow, another month has passed so quickly!

From where you are sitting, not much may have appeared to have happened this month. Songsforchoirs is in the process of moving house so we've been somewhat preoccupied with packing, organising and raiding the piggy bank for more funds. I did have an idea though. Now that the Friends and Patron system seems to be up and running we are planning to introduce a rehearsal track system so that choirs and singers can isolate their own parts on playback. We haven't figured out the details just yet but we are considering possible options.

You will have noticed the new covers for the works. We are tryiing to build and maintain our brand so that songsforchoirs becomes immediately recognisable in the general market.

Featured Work

We only have a couple of new works for you this month.

Apple Wassail  is now available. This arrangement of a traditional English folk-song is written with new choirs in mind. An experienced choir will learn this in a heartbeat. 3 verses, one mostly unison followed by a 3 part canon and finally, for the last verse, a 3 part harmonisation for SAB choir.

Summer is acomin in  is now available. Another arrangement of an ancient song. Perhaps the earliest notated part song that we are aware of. Originally written as a canon. We have tried to make it just a little bit more interesting bearing in mind that the original version only ends when the last singer gives up! We hope our version keeps the audience interested enough to stay until the end.

Thank you for joining us on the beginning of this adventure.
