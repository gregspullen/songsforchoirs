This Month at Songs for Choirs

Hello Choristers,

Welcome to songsforchoirs.This is the first chance I've had to write a column.

The last few months have been taken up with coding, testing and more coding and only thanks to Lyra has the site reached the point where I can begin to relax and allow the site to do it's thing. We believe that we have created a unique publishing system to give you the best possible experience. Now all we have to do is give you some music that is rewarding and fun to sing. I think that's easier than writing a single page of code. Lyra would disagree.

In between coding sessions we have managed to write a handful of new works. Normally we would expect to produce one or two pieces each month but as this is our start-up there are a few extras.

Featured Work

Christmas comes early at songsforchoirs as we prepare for the September onslaught of requests fof choirs preparing seasonal programs and we don't want to disappoint. The office tree helps set the mood of course even though no one can get through the door without knocking off a few baubles. As a result we have a number of imaginative new works for your Christmas programs or services.

Joseph's Prayer  is now available We wanted to write a song about Joseph, the unsung hero of the Nativity. Joseph's Prayer uses the words from Psalm 132 to express his confusion but also joy at the birth of Jesus. This could fit equally well into an Advent or Christmas event.

Gabriel: Non Erit Impossibile  is now available This was an interesting project for us. The intention was to portray an archangel who didn't know how terrifying he was to a human but also an angel who was becoming rather too excited by the message He was delivering. We decided to omit any narrative and only use Gabriel's words to put the audience/congregation in the place of Mary. So please, go and terrify your audience in Latin.
